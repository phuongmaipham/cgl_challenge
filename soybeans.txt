Soybean-only signal experiments
===============================

Run date: 2026-05-01

Purpose
-------
Test soybean-only strategies in two separate tracks:

1. Given-data-only signals from the provided training files.
2. External signals from free sources:
   - yfinance soybean complex and macro futures/FX proxies;
   - Meteostat crop-belt weather, including HDD/CDD-style features.

The notebook now has two separate optional soybean cells:

1. Soybean Given-Data-Only Signal Cell
2. Soybean External Signal Family Cell

Important caveat
----------------
These tests are lower-overfit, not impossible-to-overfit. The formulas use fixed
economic signs, equal family weights, smoothing, thresholding, and lagged
volatility targeting. However, choosing the best candidate after viewing the
research table is still a research-selection risk. The strongest conclusion
should be validated on a true 2021-2026 holdout before being called robust.

Shared backtest setup
---------------------
- Instrument: SOYABEAN front adjusted futures from the provided dataset.
- Test/OOS period: 2018-01-01 to 2020-12-31.
- Positions are long-only unless explicitly marked long/short.
- Signals are tanh-normalized, EWMA-smoothed with halflife 2, and thresholded.
- Position sizing uses lagged 60-day soybean futures PnL volatility.
- Target daily PnL volatility = 75 USD.
- Max position = 0.50 lots.
- Cost-adjusted results use 8.75 USD per one-way lot plus 5% annual margin
  funding.

Track 2 — External signals
==========================

External families tested
------------------------
1. external_crush_family
   - yfinance soybean futures, soybean meal futures, and soybean oil futures.
   - Builds a practical crush proxy:
       crush = 0.022 * soymeal + 0.11 * soyoil - soybean / 100
   - Also includes meal and oil leadership versus soybeans.

2. external_relative_grain_family
   - soybean/corn, soybean/wheat, and corn/soy relative moves.

3. external_fx_export_family
   - DXY, BRL, and CNY moves.
   - Stronger USD, weaker BRL, or weaker CNY are treated as bearish for US
     soybeans.

4. external_macro_risk_family
   - crude oil, equities, and USD trend as macro/risk appetite proxies.

5. external_weather_hdd_cdd_family
   - Meteostat crop-belt weather.
   - Includes CDD, HDD, GDD, heat stress, dryness, dry CDD, planting-season
     precipitation/dryness, and harvest freeze stress.

6. external_equal_family_weight
   - Equal weight across all available external families.

7. given_plus_external_equal_family_50_50
   - 50% given conservative soybean blend.
   - 50% equal-weight external family blend.

8. given_and_external_all_families_equal_weight
   - Equal weight across given price, given physical, and all external
     families.

9. given_crush_plus_weather_hdd_cdd_equal_weight
   - 50% given Cargill crush pressure.
   - 50% external Meteostat HDD/CDD/weather family.

10. given_conservative_plus_weather_hdd_cdd_equal_weight
    - 50% given conservative soybean blend.
    - 50% external Meteostat HDD/CDD/weather family.

11. given_physical_external_fundamentals_equal_weight
    - Equal weight across given physical, yfinance crush, FX/export, and
      weather families.

Overfit-control revision
========================

Problem
-------
The high-performing external family models still had research-selection risk:
we tested multiple external families and saw that crush + weather worked best
on the 2018-2020 test period. That improves backtest performance, but it can
overstate robustness.

Revision
--------
Add a stricter family selection rule that uses only pre-2018 information:

1. Mandatory family:
   - given_physical_family
   - This satisfies the exercise requirement because it uses both Cargill
     datasets:
       - cgl_inv via cgl_inventory_change
       - cgl_crush via crush_surprise and crush_utilization

2. Optional external families:
   - external_crush_family
   - external_fx_export_family
   - external_weather_hdd_cdd_family

3. Include an optional external family only if:
   - train Sharpe before 2016 is positive;
   - validation Sharpe from 2016-2017 is positive;
   - validation max drawdown is better than -250.

4. Equal-weight the mandatory family and all selected optional families.

Only FX/export survived the pre-2018 stability filter. The overfit-controlled

The overfit-controlled version has lower OOS Sharpe and worse drawdown than the

Present two soybean models, clearly labeled:

2. Higher-performing researched model:

       given_physical_external_fundamentals_equal_weight

   Use this as an upside research candidate, but disclose that it has higher
   research-selection risk because weather and external crush did not pass the
   stricter pre-2018 filter.

Regime-shift tests
==================

Motivation
----------
Fixed weights can still feel like research-selected parameters. I therefore
tested observable regime rules inspired by:

    hybrid_signal = (1 - trend_w) * source_signal + trend_w * trend_signal

where source_signal is an equal-weight family basket and trend_w is driven by
observable soybean volatility or trend state.

Regime definitions
------------------
- Soybean realized vol = lagged 60-day standard deviation of soybean futures
  dollar PnL.
- Long-run vol = expanding median of that realized vol.
- Low vol = realized vol < 0.80 * long-run vol.
- High vol = realized vol > 1.20 * long-run vol or above its expanding 75th
  percentile.
- Trend positive = given_trend > 0.

Regime diagnostics
------------------
Pre-2018:
- low-vol share: 26.6%
- high-vol share: 18.0%
- trend-positive share: 41.1%

2018-2020 OOS:
- low-vol share: 74.6%
- high-vol share: 0.0%
- trend-positive share: 55.1%

Regime candidates tested
------------------------
1. regime_hybrid_vol_trend_weight
   - source_signal = equal weight of given physical, FX/export, external crush,
     and weather/HDD/CDD.
   - trend_signal = equal weight of given trend and macro/risk.
   - trend_w = 0.20 normally, 0.60 in high-vol regimes.

2. regime_hybrid_trend_confirmed_weight
   - same source and trend signals.
   - trend_w = 0.60 when high-vol or trend-positive, else 0.20.

3. regime_low_physical_high_trend_switch
   - high vol: trend + FX/export + external crush.
   - otherwise: given physical + weather.

4. regime_low_vol_physical_else_balanced
   - low vol: given physical + weather.
   - otherwise: broad equal source basket.

5. regime_high_vol_fx_trend_else_physical_weather
   - high vol: trend + FX/export + external crush.
   - otherwise: given physical + weather.

Final fund-usable blend tests
=============================

Question
--------
Can we mix the best ideas together while keeping the strategy usable for a fund?

Constraints
-----------
- Cargill signals must be used.
- Avoid coefficient fitting and OOS-selected parameters.
- Prefer pre-2018 selection logic.
- Maximize the Sharpe / drawdown tradeoff after costs.
- Keep the final strategy explainable and implementable.

Fund candidates tested
----------------------
I blended the two strongest robust ideas:

1. regime_hybrid_vol_trend_weight
   - better Sharpe and fuller economic story;
   - worse drawdown.

2. drawdown_priority_pre2018_selection_physical_40_fx_20_extcrush_20_weather_20
   - better drawdown;
   - slightly lower Sharpe.

Equal weight vs family-based weight
-----------------------------------
The evidence says:

1. Broad equal weight is not best.
   - external_equal_family_weight was weak.
   - all-family equal weight diluted the useful soybean-specific signals.

2. Narrow economic equal weight can work.
   - Cargill crush + weather equal weight produced very strong OOS performance,
     but it does not include Cargill inventory, so it is not requirement-complete
     if both Cargill datasets must be in the final strategy.

3. Family-based / regime-based weighting is better for a fund-usable strategy.
   - It keeps Cargill inventory and crush in the model.
   - It gives a clearer economic allocation across physical, FX/export, crush,
     weather, and trend.
   - It allows a risk/Sharpe tradeoff to be selected before the OOS period.

Final recommendation
--------------------
Do not use the fund blend as the final model. It adds complexity without
improving the efficient frontier.

Use:

1. Main strategy for best Sharpe:

       regime_hybrid_vol_trend_weight

   Cost-adjusted OOS Sharpe 2.298, OOS DD -96.

2. Conservative strategy for best drawdown:

       drawdown_priority_pre2018_selection_physical_40_fx_20_extcrush_20_weather_20

   Cost-adjusted OOS Sharpe 2.168, OOS DD -55.

For a fund presentation, I would lead with the regime hybrid and include the
drawdown-priority version as the risk-controlled variant. If the investor is
very drawdown-sensitive, flip that ordering.
